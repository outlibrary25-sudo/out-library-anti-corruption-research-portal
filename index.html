Key Insights for Researchers
  • Magufuli Era (2016-2021): Consistent year-over-year improvement from 32 to 39, driven by high-profile dismissals, procurement reforms, and PCCB enforcement.
  • Peak Performance (2022): Score of 40 and rank of 83 represented Tanzania's best CPI performance since the index began in 1995.
  • Post-2022 Decline: Score dropped to 38 in 2024 amid concerns over political financing, infrastructure procurement, and judicial independence.
  • Regional Context: Tanzania consistently outperforms the Sub-Saharan Africa average (33) but remains below the global average (43).
  • Research Applications: Use this data for econometric analysis, policy evaluation studies, comparative governance research, and anti-corruption intervention assessments.

Source: Transparency International Corruption Perceptions Index (CPI). Score range: 0 (highly corrupt) to 100 (very clean). Tanzania's score improved from 33 (2015) to a peak of 40 (2022), representing a 21% improvement over 7 years, before declining to 38 in 2024. The CPI aggregates data from 13 sources including the World Bank, African Development Bank, Bertelsmann Foundation, Economist Intelligence Unit, and Freedom House.
